Communication

Theory

According to James Hamilton, communication is influenced by three factors, each of which influences a person's communication style differently. This scale measures the Locus of control while communicating, through 17 items. Each item contains a statement describing the general feelings about public speaking in particular and communication in general. Respondents rate how accurate these feelings and thoughts are, on a four-point scale from Strongly Disagree to Strongly Agree.

Meaning of factor

Communication refers to the imparting or exchanging of information by speaking, writing, or using some other medium. According to James Hamilton, the communication style is controlled by three factors namely,

Luck

The degree to which a person believes that luck affects their interactions and communication style is referred to as Luck in the Communication Locus of control.

Self Control

The degree to which a person believes that he/she has influence and control over himself/ herself while engaging in conversations is referred to as self-control.

Social System Control

The degree to which a person believes that environmental or societal circumstances affect their interactions and communication style is referred to as social system control.
