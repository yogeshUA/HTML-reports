Psychological wellbeing

Theory

According to C. D. Ruff, we have different perceptions and basis for measuring our well-being. This scale quantifies the level of such six innate measures through 18 items. Each item contains a statement describing the general feelings experienced towards self. Respondents rate how accurate these feelings and thoughts are, on a seven-point scale from Strongly Disagree to Strongly Agree.

Meaning of factor

Psychological Well-being is a theoretically grounded instrument that specifically focuses on measuring multiple facets of psychological well-being. These facets include the following

Autonomy

A sense of authority and self-determination and evaluating oneself according to self-imposed criteria rather than looking for social approval.

Environmental Mastery

Ability to choose and create environments suitable to one’s psychic conditions.

Personal Growth

Actualizing oneself and realizing one's potential.

Positive Relations with Others

Development and maintenance of warm and trusting relations with others.

Purpose in Life

Finding meaning and purpose in one’s past and present life, typically through the realization of goals and objectives.
