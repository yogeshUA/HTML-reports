Learning

Theory

According to Honey and Mumford, we have an inclination toward a specific style of learning, most natural to us, based on how we think about ourselves and the world. This questionnaire measures four learning styles through 40 items. Each item contains a statement describing the thoughts and approaches while trying something new. Respondents respond whether they relate to the behavior described in each statement by selecting Yes or No.

Meaning of factors

The Learning Styles Questionnaire was developed to identify the preferred learning style and quicken the training process without compromising the quality of learning.

Activist

Involve themselves fully and without bias in new experiences. Their philosophy is: "I'll try anything once."

Reflector

Like to stand back to ponder experiences and observe them from many different perspectives. Their philosophy is “To be cautious.”

Theorist

Adapt and integrate observations into complex but logically sound theories. Their philosophy is "If it’s logical it's good."

Pragmatist

Keen on trying out ideas, theories, and techniques to see if they work in practice. Their philosophy is "There is always a better way" and "If it works it's good."
