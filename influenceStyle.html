Influencing styles audit

Theory

According to Dave Francis, we have one or two influencing styles that feel most natural to us based on how we think about ourselves and the world. This audit measures nine influencing styles through 36 items. Each item contains a statement describing the process, behavior or motive while interacting with others. Respondents rate how often they indulge in the behavior described in each statement on a five-point scale from 0 (I never do this) to 4 (I always do this.)

Meaning of factor

The Influencing Styles Audit was developed to understand one’s preferred style of influencing others, using nine styles of influencing.

Value-Driven Style

Aligns the message with the values and beliefs of those to be influenced

Goal Setting Style

Clearly communicating the specific goals behind the influence

Need Fulfillment Style

Identify and address the needs of those to be influenced

Visioning Style

Painting a picture of a positive future to influence

Rational Presentation Style

Utilizing logical arguments, facts, and evidence to influence
