Problem Solving

Problem-Solving measures the incoming candidates’ and/or existing employees' approach toward solving problems. It helps the user obtain more profound and precise insights into their confidence to resolve issues.

Problem solving

Theory

According to S. Sanatkar and M. Rubin, we have an inclination towards one of the two approaches to solving a problem that feels most natural to us based on how we think about ourselves and the world. This questionnaire measures two problem-solving approaches through 10 items. Each item contains a statement describing the situation and behavior while dealing with problems. Respondents rate how accurate these statements are to their personnel behavior on a seven-point scale from Strongly Disagree to Strongly Agree.

Meaning of factors

The problem-solving questionnaire was developed to understand one’s preferred style of solving problems, using two approaches:

Independent Problem-Solving

The tendency to identify and solve problems without needing assistance or guidance from others.

Interdependent Problem-Solving

The tendency to work effectively with others to solve problems.
