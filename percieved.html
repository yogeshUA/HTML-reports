Percieved Stress

Theory

According to A. Shahid et al, we have a tendency to either undermine or amplify our sense of stress based on how we think about ourselves and the world. This questionnaire measures this level of perceived stress through 30 items. Each item contains a statement describing the situation and feelings experienced about the daily hassles. Respondents rate how often they experience these feelings and thoughts on a four-point scale from Rarely to Usually.

Meaning of factor

Perceived stress refers to an individual's subjective perception of the degree to which situations are appraised as stressful. It is a measure of how much stress a person feels they are under and is determined by their personal perception of the demands placed on them, their ability to cope with those demands, and their emotional and physiological responses to those demands.
