Career adapt-abilities

Measure the applicants’ and/or existing employees’ ability to adapt to changes in a timely manner while coping with uncertainty and ambiguity. Career adaptability describes four adapt-abilities that workers can develop and utilize to address career challenges and transitions.

Career adapt-abilities

Theory

According to Kevin Glavin and Carolyn Berger, different people have varying tendencies to use different strengths to build their careers, based on how we think about ourselves and the world. No one is good at everything, each of us emphasizes some strengths more than others.

Meaning of factors

Employees’ capabilities to adapt to rapidly changing work situations include problem- solving, coping with uncertainty, exploring different vocational areas, and learning new tasks and procedures.
