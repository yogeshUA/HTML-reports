MEWP-SF

Theory

According to M. J. Miller, D. J. Woehr, and N. Hudspeth, there are seven different dimensions of work ethics that each individual is inclined towards based on how we think about ourselves and the world. This questionnaire measures these dimensions through 28 items. Each item contains a statement describing the thoughts, feeling or behavior while working. Respondents rate how often they indulge in the behavior described in each statement on a five-point scale from Strongly Disagree to Strongly Agree.

Meaning of factor

The MEWP-SF was developed using seven dimensions of ethics to shed light on the employees' attitudes and behavior with respect to work.

Centrality of work

Believe in work for work’s sake and the importance of work

Self-reliance

Striving for independence in one’s daily work

Hard work

Believe in the virtues of hard work

Leisure

Pro leisure attitudes and believe in the importance of nonwork activities

Morality/ethics

Believe in a just and moral existence
