Coping Styles

Theory

According to S. Joseph, R. Williams, and W. Yule, we have an inclination toward specific coping strategies, most natural to us, based on how we think about ourselves and the world. This questionnaire measures three coping strategies through 30 items. Each item contains a statement describing the thoughts and behavior while being burdened. Respondents rate how often they indulge in the behavior described in each statement on a four-point scale from I usually don't do this at all to I usually do this a lot.

Avoidant coping

A strategy where one avoids dealing with stressors, difficulties, or unpleasant emotions by retreating, withdrawing, or ignoring them.

Emotion-focused coping

A strategy that focuses on acknowledging and addressing the emotions elicited by a stressor followed by managing and regulating them.

Task-focused coping

A strategy where one focuses on taking concrete and direct actions to solve or manage a stressor.
