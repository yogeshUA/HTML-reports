Coping Styles

Theory

According to James Hamilton, communication is influenced by three factors, each of which influences a person's communication style differently. This scale measures the Locus of control while communicating, through 17 items. Each item contains a statement describing the general feelings about public speaking in particular and communication in general. Respondents rate how accurate these feelings and thoughts are, on a four-point scale from Strongly Disagree to Strongly Agree.

Meaning of factors

The Coping Style Questionnaire was developed to understand one’s preferred strategy to get through difficult situations.

Avoidant coping

A strategy where one avoids dealing with stressors, difficulties, or unpleasant emotions by retreating, withdrawing, or ignoring them.

Emotion-focused coping

A strategy that focuses on acknowledging and addressing the emotions elicited by a stressor followed by managing and regulating them.

Task-focused coping

A strategy where one focuses on taking concrete and direct actions to solve or manage a stressor.
