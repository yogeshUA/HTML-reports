I-ADAPT-M

Theory

According to R. Ployhart, and P. Bliese, in current times, employees need to exhibit adaptability in ideas, values, and behavior. This scale quantifies the level of eight such adaptability areas through 55 items. Each item contains a statement describing the general feelings experienced towards self and work. Respondents rate how accurate these feelings and thoughts are, on a five-point scale from Strongly Disagree to Strongly Agree.

Meaning of factor

The I-ADAPT-M was developed using eight dimensions of adaptive performance to help identify the strengths of the applicants/ employees.

Handling Emergency or Crisis Situations

Reacting with appropriate and proper urgency in life-threatening, dangerous, or emergency situations.

Handling Work Stress

Remaining composed and cool when faced with difficult circumstances or a highly demanding workload or schedule.

Handling Work Stress

Employing unique types of analyses and generating new, innovative ideas in complex areas.

Dealing with Uncertain and Unpredictable Work Situations

Taking effective action when necessary without knowing the full picture or having all facts at hand.
