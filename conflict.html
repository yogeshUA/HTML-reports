Conflict Management

Theory

According to Adkins Reginald[2], the techniques used to deal with conflicts are based on many variables such as our basic underlying temperament, personality, environment, and where we are in our professional career.

Validity and reliability

Cronbach’s coefficient alpha for the conflict management styles questionnaire was found to be highly reliable (α = 0.86), and factor analyses supported the factor structure of the scale.

Meaning of factor

The Conflict Management Styles can be clubbed together into the following five major styles. None of these strategies are superior in and of itself.

Collaborating

Working through differences will lead to creative solutions that will satisfy both parties' concerns.

Avoiding

Avoiding conflict by withdrawing, sidestepping, or postponing.

Compromising

Both ends are placed against the middle in an attempt to serve the "common good," while ensuring each person can maintain something of their original position.
